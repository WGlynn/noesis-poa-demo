    04 — the nash equilibrium

    Lying doesn't pay: honesty is the best response

    HL asked: what if I pretend to be someone, or lie about a contribution? Here is every move and what it earns. Payoff = allocation from the measure minus expected slash: u = φ(v) − ℓ.

    your movewhat the protocol doespayoff
    contribute honestlyscored by realized downstream value, earn standingEARN
    pretend to be someone ("I'm Vitalik")a fresh identity has no history, can't inherit or back-date priority, worth 00
    claim work that isn't yours / lie about itbonded self-report; caught with prob p, bond slashed + over-claim clawed backLOSE
    pad / paraphrase / copytemporal novelty of duplicate work, 00

    → only honesty has a positive payoff, so honest contribution is the unique best response. That is the Nash equilibrium.

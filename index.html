    05 — the finder's reward

    Surface someone else's work, and get paid for it

    You don't have to be the author to add value — finding and submitting real contributions is work too. So anyone can surface a contribution (paste a URL in panel 02), and the protocol splits two different rewards down two different axes:

    whoearnskind
    the author (@ from the source)standing, priced by realized valueSOULBOUND
    you, the findera cut of that realized valueTRANSFERABLE

    Keeping them on separate axes is the whole trick: the finder is paid in the fungible layer, never in the author's soulbound authority — so surfacing work can never buy franchise. And the reward only pays if the work clears the novelty floor, so spamming duplicates to farm finder fees earns 0. The mechanism you already saw does the policing.

    06 — provenance

    Provenance is an airgap problem — and honesty is what closes it

    The chain can't see who made something, or when, out in the world. The tempting fix is to reach across the gap — fetch it from a GitHub or Reddit API and trust their word. That just relocates trust to an oracle. Noesis does the opposite: it makes lying about provenance unprofitable, so it never has to reach across.

    the engine — the on-chain commit is the native timestamp (content hash + block height + identity). Claiming someone else's earlier work is a losing bet: a fresh identity is worth 0, priority can't be back-dated, and a false claim is a bonded self-report — caught, the bond is slashed and the over-claim clawed back. (that's panel 04, on the provenance axis.)
    stronger proof doesn't create trust — it just raises the odds a lie is caught, lowering the enforcement cost. pick what the submitter brings:
    rungun-forgeable bywhen it applies
    on-chain commit / hash rootcollision resistancenative — the commit itself
    Pragma coherence / observer-overlapredundancy, code-distanceno crypto timestamp available
    bare claimnothing (priority from commit onward)last resort

    Pragma is additive, not a dependency. The incentive structure closes the airgap on its own; Pragma raises the catch-probability (many independent observers) and hands the agent an external credential. Cryptographic (Noesis) and redundancy-based (Pragma) integrity — same goal, two threat models. Nothing here holds a key.

    the honest edge: this holds against rational unilateral and cyclic lies. A symmetric collusion ring — many observers agreeing on the same false timestamp — is the open frontier (the same one panel 04 names). Pragma raises the cost of assembling that ring; it doesn't yet make it impossible. We show the boundary.

    This is the airgap Noesis and VibeSwap are built to close — on-chain trust made equal to off-chain trust by making dishonesty unprofitable. It is the same property Tom's "trusted agent chain" is reaching for, seen from the substrate side rather than the certifier side.

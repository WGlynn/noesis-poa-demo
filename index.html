    04 — the finder's reward

    Surface someone else's work, and get paid for it

    Two rewards, two axes — the author's soulbound standing and the finder's transferable cut:

    whoearnskind

    why it's safe

    Separate axes is the trick: the finder is paid in the fungible layer, never the author's soulbound authority — so surfacing can't buy franchise. And no reward unless the work clears the novelty floor, so farming duplicates earns 0.

    one action, two outcomes — claiming your own work and surfacing someone else's are the same share, the ordinary share button every app already has. The protocol tells them apart by whether your Noesis key is linked to the author's handle (panel 05): share your own → you're the author (soulbound standing); share another's → you're the finder (a lottery ticket).
    how the finder is paid — a 24h value-pool lottery. move the sliders, or try to farm it with a duplicate:
    the mechanism & credit

    Mechanism proposed by Maarten G (2026-07-01): let anyone share a contribution — even one that isn't theirs — through the ordinary share action, and enter the sharers of original links into a lottery for a slice of the period's realized value. Made safe here by three locks: the reward is transferable-only (never the author's soulbound authority); tickets require an original surface that clears the novelty floor; and credit routes to the author through the social-account link (panel 05). Same fair expectation as a fixed cut, with a share-and-maybe-win UX.

    where Pragma fits

    Pragma is additive, not a dependency. The incentive structure closes the airgap on its own; Pragma raises the catch-probability (many independent observers) and hands the agent an external credential. Cryptographic (Noesis) and redundancy-based (Pragma) integrity — same goal, two threat models. Nothing here holds a key.

    how far it closes

    redundancy vs. structure: a pure overlap scheme can be forged by a large enough ring of agreeing observers — that is the ceiling of redundancy alone. Noesis closes it structurally: a false timestamp cannot finalize without clearing the anti-concentration floor, so forging provenance would require a majority of earned, soulbound contribution — which capital cannot buy. What remains is only the irreducible Nakamoto bound — the genuine contributor-majority itself defecting — at which point "collusion" and "the legitimate authority deciding" are the same event. The still-open research frontier is elsewhere: the learned value function v(S) (the case panel 03 names), whose first real-data test came back null.
